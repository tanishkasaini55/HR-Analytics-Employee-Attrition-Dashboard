# HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition and workforce trends using **Excel, SQL, and Power BI**. The goal of the project is to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover and convert raw HR data into meaningful business insights that can support data-driven HR decision-making.

The dashboard was built to answer important workforce questions such as:
- Which employee groups are experiencing the highest attrition?
- How do salary, overtime, and experience affect attrition?
- Which demographic and satisfaction-related factors are associated with employee turnover?
- What business actions can help improve employee retention?

---

## Problem Statement
Employee attrition is one of the most important challenges faced by organizations because it affects productivity, recruitment costs, employee morale, and long-term workforce stability. This project aims to analyze HR data to uncover attrition patterns, identify retention risks, and provide actionable HR recommendations.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze employee attrition trends across different workforce segments
- Identify the major drivers of employee turnover
- Build an interactive HR Analytics dashboard in Power BI
- Perform data analysis using SQL and Excel
- Generate business insights and HR recommendations to improve retention

- Experience group

### 3. Employee Satisfaction & Retention Drivers
This page highlights the factors that may influence retention, including:

- Environment satisfaction

### 4. Executive Summary & Recommendations
This page summarizes the most important findings from the dashboard and presents actionable HR recommendations to reduce attrition and improve workforce retention.

---

## Key Business Insights
Some major insights from the analysis include:
- Employees in the **low salary band** show the highest attrition
- **0–2 years experience** employees are more likely to leave
- **Research & Development** has the highest attrition count
- Employees working **overtime** experience higher attrition
- **26–35 age group** shows the highest attrition
- **Work-life balance and job satisfaction** strongly influence retention

---

## HR Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following business recommendations were suggested:
- Improve work-life balance through flexible policies and workload planning
- Review salary structures for lower salary bands
- Monitor overtime and reduce workload pressure where possible
- Strengthen onboarding and engagement for early-tenure employees
- Conduct regular employee feedback and satisfaction reviews
- Use workforce analytics dashboards for continuous HR decision-making
